About

  • As the Director of the School of Computing and IT, I bring a wealth of expertise in blockchain technology, system dynamics, and the integration of assistive technology with AI. My primary goal is to facilitate collaboration between academia and industry, ensuring that our research leads to tangible applications and real-world solutions.
  • Within my role, I am deeply committed to supporting student engagement and academic development, particularly in the domains of blockchain, system dynamics, and assistive technology. I work closely with students, faculty, and industry partners to provide mentorship, resources, and opportunities for research and learning.
  • I play a central role in managing and overseeing events and resources related to blockchain technology, system dynamics, and assistive technology integrated with AI. This includes organizing seminars, workshops, and conferences aimed at fostering collaboration and innovation in these rapidly evolving fields. Beyond academia, I am passionate about exploring the diverse applications of blockchain, system dynamics, and assistive technology with AI. By leveraging my expertise, I aim to contribute to advancements that positively impact various sectors, from healthcare to finance and beyond.
