As a dedicated educator in the Department of Computing at JKUAT, I am committed to providing a comprehensive and engaging learning experience for my students. My teaching philosophy emphasizes practical application and critical thinking, ensuring that students not only grasp theoretical concepts but also develop the skills to implement them in real-world scenarios.

About

  • I have extensive expertise in several advanced and emerging technology domains, including:
    Artificial Intelligence (AI): Proficient in developing intelligent systems capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as visual perception, speech recognition, decision-making, and language translation.
    Machine Learning (ML): Skilled in designing, implementing, and deploying machine learning models for predictive analytics, classification, clustering, and recommendation systems.
    Data Science: Experienced in extracting insights and knowledge from structured and unstructured data through statistical analysis, data mining, and visualization techniques.
  • I strive to create an interactive and inclusive classroom environment that fosters curiosity and innovation. Through a combination of lectures, hands-on projects, and mentorship, I aim to inspire my students to explore the frontiers of technology and make significant contributions to the field.
  • My research interests are centered around leveraging advanced technologies to solve complex problems and enhance user interaction with systems. One of my key research projects involves the:
    Design of a Natural Language Front-End to a Relational Database: This research aims to develop an intuitive interface that allows users to interact with relational databases using natural language queries. By bridging the gap between human language and database query languages, this project seeks to make data access and manipulation more accessible to non-technical users.
    Through this research, I am exploring innovative ways to integrate AI and natural language processing (NLP) with database technologies, ultimately aiming to improve the usability and accessibility of data systems
